Indicator overview

BF-NEE measures natural ecosystem area loss associated with agricultural land expansion and attributes this loss to commodity consumption.

The indicator combines:

  1. LandSHIFT agricultural land-use information
  2. GlobES ecosystem extent information
  3. Commodity-specific consumption shares
  4. GLORIA production attribution shares

GlobES and LandSHIFT harmonisation

Raw GlobES layers represent ecosystem cover by IUCN ecosystem class.

In the workflow, GlobES ecosystem shares are first aligned to the LandSHIFT grid. Agricultural land from LandSHIFT is then used to adjust the GlobES ecosystem composition so that the agricultural share in GlobES matches LandSHIFT.

LandSHIFT agricultural area is coupled to the following GlobES/IUCN ecosystem classes:

Operational definition of natural ecosystems

In the BF-NEE workflow, natural ecosystems are defined operationally as:

All GlobES/IUCN ecosystem classes remaining after excluding the specified non-natural classes, with agricultural classes handled separately through LandSHIFT adjustment.

More specifically:

  • agricultural classes: 14.1, 15.8
  • excluded non-natural classes: 14.4, 14.5, 15.4, 15.5, 15.6, 15.11
  • natural ecosystem loss is calculated from the remaining natural ecosystem classes

Ecosystem adjustment procedure

The adjustment proceeds in three steps.

Step 1: Remove excluded classes

The excluded classes are set to zero.

The remaining ecosystem shares are renormalized so that the non-excluded classes sum to one.

Step 2: Match agriculture to LandSHIFT

The total GlobES agricultural share is calculated as the sum of classes 14.1 and 15.8.

This agricultural share is replaced by the LandSHIFT agricultural share.

Step 3: Rescale remaining ecosystems

All non-agricultural ecosystem classes are rescaled so that the full ecosystem composition again sums to one.

Area calculation

For each grid cell, ecosystem-class area is calculated as:

[ A_{p,e,y} = share_{p,e,y} cellArea_p ]

where:

  • (A_{p,e,y}) is the area of ecosystem class (e) in pixel (p) and year (y)
  • (share_{p,e,y}) is the adjusted ecosystem share
  • (cellArea_p) is the LandSHIFT cell area in km²

Natural ecosystem loss

Change in ecosystem extent is calculated between consecutive time steps:

[ dA_{p,e,t} = A_{p,e,t} - A_{p,e,t-1} ]

Only losses of natural ecosystems are retained:

[ lossA_{p,e,t} = max(-dA_{p,e,t}, 0) ]

The total natural ecosystem loss in a pixel is:

[ lossA_{p,t} = e lossA{p,e,t} ]

where the sum is taken over natural ecosystem classes.

Commodity attribution

For each commodity, the attribution weight is calculated as:

[ W_{c,r,y} = S_{c,r,y} P_{c,r,y} ]

where:

  • (S_{c,r,y}) is the commodity share associated with consumption
  • (P_{c,r,y}) is the GLORIA production attribution share
  • (W_{c,r,y}) is the commodity-specific attribution weight

BF-NEE calculation

Pixel-level BF-NEE is calculated as:

[ BFNEE_{p,t} = lossA_{p,t} c W{c,r,y} ]

The unit of BF-NEE is:

[ km^2 ]

Interpretation

BF-NEE represents consumption-attributed natural ecosystem loss.

A value of 10 km² means that, according to the attribution scheme, 10 km² of natural ecosystem loss in that location and transition period are associated with the considered consumption demand.

# Area calculation

For each grid cell, ecosystem-class area is calculated as:

\[
A_{p,e,y}
=
share_{p,e,y}
\times
cellArea_p
\]

where:

- \(A_{p,e,y}\) is the area of ecosystem class \(e\) in pixel \(p\) and year \(y\)
- \(share_{p,e,y}\) is the adjusted ecosystem share
- \(cellArea_p\) is the LandSHIFT cell area in km²

# Natural ecosystem loss

Change in ecosystem extent is calculated between consecutive time steps:

\[
dA_{p,e,t}
=
A_{p,e,t}
-
A_{p,e,t-1}
\]

Only losses of natural ecosystems are retained:

\[
lossA_{p,e,t}
=
max(-dA_{p,e,t}, 0)
\]

The total natural ecosystem loss in a pixel is:

\[
lossA_{p,t}
=
\sum_e lossA_{p,e,t}
\]

where the sum is taken over natural ecosystem classes.

# Commodity attribution

For each commodity, the attribution weight is calculated as:

\[
W_{c,r,y}
=
S_{c,r,y}
\times
P_{c,r,y}
\]

where:

- \(S_{c,r,y}\) is the commodity share associated with consumption
- \(P_{c,r,y}\) is the GLORIA production attribution share
- \(W_{c,r,y}\) is the commodity-specific attribution weight

# BF-NEE calculation

Pixel-level BF-NEE is calculated as:

\[
BF\text{-}NEE_{p,t}
=
lossA_{p,t}
\times
\sum_c W_{c,r,y}
\]

The unit of BF-NEE is:

\[
km^2
\]

A value of 10 km² means that, according to the attribution scheme, 10 km² of natural ecosystem loss in that location and transition period are associated with the considered consumption demand.
